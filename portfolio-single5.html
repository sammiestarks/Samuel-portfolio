Enhancing Transaction History and Statement Generation for First Bank Mobile App

Overview

The First Bank Mobile App is a critical tool for millions of users who depend on it for seamless financial transactions. However, recurring user complaints highlight significant transaction history visibility and statement generation challenges. Customers frequently report missing recent transactions, failure to load transaction history, and difficulties generating receipts after exiting the app.

Beyond functional limitations, the visuals and UI also require a revamp to improve clarity, accessibility, and overall user experience. This case study aims to analyze key pain points, validate them with user feedback, and propose UX/UI improvements that enhance usability, ensure better financial tracking, and create a more intuitive interface. Through usability research, competitive analysis, and design iteration, the goal is to modernize the app’s transaction experience while maintaining First Bank’s brand identity and financial service standards.

Design Process Overview

Our design process for revamping the transaction history and statement generation on the First Bank mobile app followed a structured, user-centered approach:

1User Feedback Analysis – We reviewed App Store and Play Store reviews to identify real user frustrations and validate the need for improvements.

2Persona Development – We created personas based on common pain points to understand user needs better, ensuring our solutions were tailored to real-world banking behaviors.

3Cognitive Walkthrough – We simulated user journeys to identify usability issues firsthand, uncovering challenges such as hidden transaction history, inconsistent icons, and overloaded information.

4Heuristic Evaluation – We mapped out UI/UX issues using Jakob Nielsen’s usability principles to pinpoint areas needing better navigation, consistency, and clarity.

5Competitive Analysis – We analyzed Opay, a highly praised fintech app, to gather insights on best practices for handling transaction history and statement generation.

6Sketching & Ideation – With insights from previous steps, we sketched potential solutions to enhance usability, accessibility, and information hierarchy.

7High-Fidelity Design – We transformed our sketches into polished, pixel-perfect UI designs, incorporating improved navigation, filtering, and statement generation workflows.

8Usability Testing & Survey – A remote usability test was conducted by sharing a prototype and survey to collect feedback, which helped refine our final design.

This structured approach ensured our redesign was data-driven, user-focused, and aligned with best UX/UI practices, leading to a more intuitive and seamless banking experience.

Discovering the Problem: Listening to the Users

To truly understand the challenges users faced with transaction history and statement generation, we went straight to the source—real customer feedback.

By reviewing user complaints on the Apple App Store and Google Play Store, we uncovered a pattern of frustrations that highlighted major usability issues:

  • Transaction history not loading - Users reported that their transaction records failed to appear, making it impossible to track past payments.
  • Missing transactions - Several complaints pointed out that the app only displayed the last 20 days of transactions, leaving users unable to access older records.
  • Issues with receipt generation - Many users noted that once they exited the app after making a payment, they couldn’t retrieve a receipt when they logged back in.
  • CLuttered and outdated UI - Beyond functionality, users struggled with poor visual hierarchy and unclear navigation, making it difficult to find key financial details.

To truly understand how frustrating the transaction history and statement generation experience was, we conducted a cognitive walkthrough—a step-by-step simulation of how our personas would navigate the First Bank Mobile App. This helped us identify usability gaps, friction points, and inefficiencies in the current experience.

Heuristic Evaluation: Mapping Out UI/UX Issues

Before diving into the competitive analysis, we conducted a heuristic evaluation to systematically identify usability issues in First Bank’s transaction history and statement generation process. This helped us pinpoint key UI/UX problems based on Jakob Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics.

Issues

  • Icons are inconsistent
  • Information Overload
  • Transaction history is linked on the active card. Users have to scroll to the active card to see the transaction history of that card. (Users are not aware)

Recommendation

  • Make use of an icon set to ensure consistency
  • Remove the my ADs title
  • De-emphasis the banks color
  • Rework the statistics

Issues

  • Date Range toggle is ineffective
  • Inconsistent white space
  • Apply and Email Statement exist as 2 separate actions
  • Account being filtered is unknown

Recommendation

  • Remove the "Use Date Range"
  • Make white space consistent
  • Use a checkbox for the email statement
  • Account being filtered should also be displayed. Use a dropdown to switch accounts

Issues

  • Transaction history for specified account is not specified. Users can't tell which history is displayed
  • Calendar icon is used for both transaction history and filter
  • User can mistakenly tap any of the navigation button and be redirected to another page

Recommendation

  • Show the specified account
  • Allow users switch between accounts and display all if necessary
  • Use a filter icon for filter
  • Remove the navigation bar to allow the user focus on the goal

What We Learned

The cognitive walkthrough validated what users had been complaining about:

1Inconsistent Icons – The use of inconsistent icons across the app creates confusion and reduces usability.

2Overload of Information – The transaction history screen presents too much information at once, making it overwhelming and difficult to scan.

3Hidden Transaction History – Users must scroll to the active card to view its transaction history, but they are unaware of this functionality, leading to frustration.

4Unclear Account Association – The transaction history does not specify which account it belongs to, confusing, especially when recent transactions are missing.

5Misuse of Calendar Icon – The same calendar icon is used for both transaction history and filtering, making it unclear which function it represents.

6Navigation Issues – Users can mistakenly tap a navigation button and be redirected to another page, disrupting their flow.

7Ineffective Date Range Toggle – The toggle for selecting a date range does not function properly, limiting users' ability to filter transactions effectively.

8Inconsistent White Space – The layout lacks proper spacing, making the UI feel cluttered and harder to navigate.

9Disjointed Statement Actions – The "Apply" and "Email Statement" actions exist as separate options, but users might expect to view the statement first before emailing it.

10Unknown Filtered Account – When filtering transactions, users are not shown which account is being filtered, leading to uncertainty about the results.

Competitive Analysis: Learning from Opay’s Success

To understand how a seamless transaction history and statement generation experience should work, we conducted a competitive analysis. We chose Opay as our benchmark due to the overwhelming praise it receives from social media, users, and merchants.

Why Opay?

Opay has positioned itself as one of the most user-friendly financial apps in Nigeria. Users frequently highlight its speed, reliability, and ease of use, especially when it comes to transaction history and receipt generation.

Sketching Ideas: Turning Insights into Solutions

Armed with the data from our cognitive walkthrough, heuristic evaluation, and competitive analysis, we began the ideation process by sketching out potential solutions. This phase was crucial in visualizing improvements before moving into high-fidelity designs.

From Sketches to High-Fidelity Designs

With our validated sketches in place, we moved forward with creating high-fidelity designs to bring our ideas to life. This phase focused on translating the wireframes into polished UI designs while maintaining usability and accessibility best practices.

Remote Usability Testing: Validating Our Solutions

After designing the high-fidelity prototypes, we conducted a remote usability test to gather real user feedback. Since the goal was to ensure that the new transaction history and statement generation experience was intuitive and solved the pain points, we shared a survey along with a prototype link to collect insights.

Key Insights from the Survey Responses

Users generally found navigating transaction history and generating statements intuitive (rated 4/5), but some struggled to locate older transactions. Transaction details were displayed and sufficient for verification.

One user reported difficulty accessing transaction history, and the average satisfaction rating was 8.2/10, indicating a positive experience with room for improvement.

Key Improvements Needed:

  • Make past transactions more accessible with better filters.
  • Improve discoverability of the statement generation feature.
  • Introduce a quick transaction details view for instant access.

Enhancing these areas will ensure a smoother and more user-friendly experience.

Conclusion

The First Bank mobile app’s transaction history and statement generation revamp was guided by real user pain points, heuristic evaluations, and competitive analysis. Users struggled with inconsistent UI elements, poor discoverability of key features, and difficulty accessing past transactions.

We addressed these challenges through a structured design process—from cognitive walkthroughs and usability testing to high-fidelity redesigns—by improving navigation, visual clarity, and accessibility. The revamped design ensures a seamless transaction history experience, intuitive statement generation, and a refined UI for better usability.

This transformation not only enhances efficiency and user satisfaction but also aligns the app with modern financial UX standards, making banking more accessible and frustration-free for its users.
