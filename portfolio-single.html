Sterling Pro Analytics Redesign – Improving Data Clarity & Decision-Making

Overview

I redesigned the Sterling Pro Admin Analytics dashboard to make critical insights easier to find, compare, and act on—resulting in a clearer information hierarchy, reduced visual clutter, and faster decision-making for operations teams.

About the Product

Sterling Pro is an enterprise platform used by Sterling Bank to manage onboarding, transactions, and operational performance. The Analytics section plays a critical role in providing insights that guide internal decisions across teams.

My Role

Lead UX Designer responsible for uncovering pain points, redefining the information architecture, and designing a more intuitive dashboard with scalable components.

Team & Collaboration

Worked closely with product owners, compliance officers, internal stakeholders, and the engineering team to align design with business needs and technical feasibility.

Problem / Challenge

The existing analytics dashboard made it difficult for users to extract meaningful insights because:

1Poor information hierarchy – Critical metrics were buried under redundant or low-value widgets.

2Cluttered layout with wasted space – Widgets were scattered and inconsistent, limiting scalability for future data.

3Limited insight into transaction channels – Users couldn’t track high-value trends or identify underperforming channels.

4Low data comprehension – Uniform colors, dense charts, and separate complaint views created visual overload.

Research

User & Stakeholder Interviews

Interviewed 20 users and internal stakeholders to understand:

  1. What insights they searched for most frequently
  2. Which dashboards they used the least—and why
  3. Where bottlenecks occurred in making operational decisions
Key Insights
  1. Users spent too long identifying high-value trends
  2. Important metrics were often missed due to poor labeling & layout
  3. Complaints and feature usage required excessive back-and-forth navigationch

Approach / Process

Information Architecture Redesign

Reorganized analytics into clear, high-value categories to reduce cognitive load.

Layout Restructuring

Consolidated redundant widgets, freeing space for strategic metrics such as:
  1. Transfer type performance
  2. Channel breakdown
  3. Feature usage trends
  4. Complaint analysis

Visual Design Exploration

Focused on:
  1. A clean visual hierarchy
  2. Distinct, meaningful color coding
  3. Scalable data-visualization patterns
  4. Components that can support future analytics modules

Usability Testing

Tested with 15 participants to observe:
  1. Ease of identifying key metrics
  2. Speed of navigation
  3. Accuracy of understanding visualizations

Solution

Streamlined Layout & Hierarchy
  1. Consolidated cluttered widgets into a structured, scalable grid
  2. Prioritized high-value analytics such as transfers and feature usage
  3. Improved visibility and decreased page scanning time
Transfer Channel Breakdown
  1. Added a breakdown of transaction volumes and values by channel
  2. Enabled teams to track which channels were most used and most valuable
Improved Feature Usage & Complaint Analysis
  1. Introduced unique colors for each feature
  2. Replaced multiple complaint charts with a single chart + dropdown toggle
  3. Adopted stacked bar charts for easier comparison

Outcome / Results

Better data comprehension - Users could now identify top-performing features, channels, and trends at a glance.

Reduced visual clutter - The redesign removed redundancies and improved navigability across analytics views.

Faster operational insights - Teams spent less time hunting for metrics and more time acting on insights.

Learnings

  • Understanding user roles deeply is essential in analytics design.
  • Iterative testing revealed usability issues not visible during design.
  • A strong visual hierarchy can drastically improve comprehension.
  • Toggles > multiple views for maintaining clarity.

Next Steps

  • Conduct follow-up testing on real usage data post-launch
  • Track which visualizations are most frequently used
  • Explore predictive analytics dashboards for proactive insights
  • Expand the module with more granular filters and advanced comparisons
