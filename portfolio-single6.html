Scientia: Empowering Minds with the Latest Economic Insights

Overview

I led the UX design of Scientia, a data-driven research platform created for Sterling Bank to make economic insights accessible to researchers, professionals, and students. Through user interviews, iterative design, and close collaboration with developers, we built a seamless platform offering up-to-date economic data, downloadable files, expert analysis, and an admin dashboard that powers efficient content management and long-term scalability.

Scientia was created to solve a growing gap in the research and publishing world: traditional platforms rely on outdated content, poor navigation, and limited data access. Users struggle to find deep, reliable, and timely economic information.

Client

Sterling Bank

Year

2020-present

Category

SAAS - Research and Insights

Role

Product/UX Designer responsible for research, UX architecture, visual identity development, prototyping, UI design, and design-to-development collaboration.

Problem / Challenge

Despite the abundance of news publications, there was no unified platform providing accessible, timely, and deep economic analysis. Existing platforms suffered from:

  1. Outdated research
  2. Poor categorization
  3. Limited context behind data
  4. Difficult navigation
  5. No ability to download or interact with datasets
  6. Lack of modern, user-friendly UI

Researchers, professionals, and students needed a reliable, current, easy-to-understand source of economic insights—not fragmented or outdated information.

Approach / Process

1. User Research

We conducted interviews with:

  1. Researchers
  2. Business analysts
  3. Students
Key Insights
  1. Users needed timely, trustworthy data
  2. They preferred digestible explanations alongside raw datasets
  3. Existing platforms were either too shallow (news) or too complex (academic databases)

2. Inspiration & Gap Analysis

We studied platforms like McKinsey's Featured Insights and identified two major gaps:

  1. Lack of real-time data
  2. Lack of contextual breakdowns (audio, visual, commentary)

This helped us shape Scientia into a hybrid platform: data + explanation + accessibility.

3. Ideation & Sketching

Armed with this understanding, we brainstormed ideas and sketched out designs, keeping user feedback at the forefront of our process. We weren’t just creating a platform; we were building a tool that people could rely on.

  1. How to present complex information simply
  2. How to make datasets easy to download
  3. How to make the content more engaging via podcasts
  4. How to structure categories to reduce navigation friction

Sketches explored:

  1. Article layout variations
  2. Data visualization options
  3. Category and search structures
  4. Podcast integration
  5. Admin workflows

4. High-Fidelity UI Design

We translated wireframes into polished screens focused on:

  1. Clean, modern layout
  2. Professional but accessible tone
  3. Strong typography hierarchy
  4. Friendly colors that reflect the brand’s mission
  5. Seamless integration of podcasts, datasets, and articles
  6. Intuitive patterns inspired by top SaaS content platforms

Collaboration with a graphic designer ensured a strong visual identity—logo, color palette, and UI components consistent across the platform.


5. Agile Collaboration With Developers

We worked in iterative sprints:

  1. Aligning designs with technical feasibility
  2. Ensuring content structures matched backend logic
  3. Solving integration issues for datasets and content scheduling
  4. Testing responsiveness and edge cases

Solution

A Modern Research & Insights Platform

Scientia provides:

  1. Up-to-date economic articles
  2. Downloadable datasets for researchers
  3. Podcasts that break down complex concepts
  4. A user-friendly interface designed for clarity and speed

Admin Dashboard — The Engine Behind Scientia

Designed as a command center for content teams, it provides:

Creating and Managing Content

Admins can effortlessly create and manage posts, categorizing them for easy access and adding multimedia elements to enhance the content. Whether it's scheduling posts or making quick edits, the process is straightforward and efficient.

Keeping Things Organized

Managing categories is simple, ensuring that all content is organized logically and is easy for users to find. As the platform grows, these categories can be updated to reflect new content strategies.

Results and Metrics


User Engagement

  1. Strong user registrations

  3. Increased repeat visits

Business Impact

Scientia fills a critical knowledge gap left by traditional publishing platforms.

We conducted market benchmarking while designing the platform. This required:

Conclusion

Scientia is more than a research platform—it’s a modern knowledge ecosystem designed to help people understand economic trends and make informed decisions.

By grounding our process in user research, iterative testing, and deep collaboration, we delivered a platform that is accessible, insightful, and scalable for future expansion.
